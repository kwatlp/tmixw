Origin · nɬeʔkepmxcín

tmíxʷ — the ground a story stands on.

Tmíxʷ is a foundational concept in the Nlaka'pamux language and worldview, referring broadly to the land, the earth, and all living things within it. It encompasses the collective history, resources, and spiritual connection the Nlaka'pamux people have to their traditional territories.

We chose the word because a story needs ground to stand on — a world that holds what happened, a place that remembers the people you met and the choices you made. We use the term with care. It is acknowledged language, not decoration.

The studio

kʷátɬp — cedar.

est. 2026

Cedar is the container. We build off its strength — strong, reliable, expansive, beautiful — and we put each project under it as a separate room.

i. Strong Long-grained. Doesn't crack under weather. Holds the roof up.
ii. Reliable Predictable. Each plank behaves like the last one. You can plan a house from it.
iii. Expansive Grows tall and wide. One trunk can shelter many things at once.
iv. Beautiful Quiet figure, warm tone. Doesn't have to shout to be looked at.

tmíxʷ is the first room under that roof. Other projects will follow as they're ready — each one its own thing, each one held by the same wood.
